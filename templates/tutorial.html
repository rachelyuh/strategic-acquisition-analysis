Tutorial

1. Select a buyer, or a company that you want see acquire another company. Provide the prompt with the stock ticker of that company.
2. Select a seller, or a company that you want to see bought by the company in step one. Acquivision will build a financial profile of the company behind the scenes and create a purchase price based on said profile.
3. Select how you want to finance the acquisition of company B: cash or debt. When prompted with what percentage of the purchase will be financed with debt, the rest of the purchase is assumed to be bought with cash.
4. Acquivision’s algorithm then projects the financials of both companies into the intermediate future, combining projected net incomes, subtracting interest expenses, and dividing by the new share count of the buyer after buying the seller company. This leaves us with our new EPS values.
5. The EPS values of each forecasted year are then shown. A positive EPS signals that the acquisition is accretive (good!) and a negative EPS signals that the acquisition is dilutive (bad…).
6. Finally, an explanation of the data is then presented to make sense of the numbers in a digestible format.
